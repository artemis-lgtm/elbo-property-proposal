## Combined Roadmap: Leverage + Acquisition

### Phase 1: Build the Arsenal (Now → End of 2027)
*Everything loaded and ready. Nothing filed or launched yet.*

| # | Task | Why |
|---|------|-----|
| 1 | **Hire CRE attorney** (Broward) | Quarterbacks the entire strategy — acquisitions, preservation law, lease dynamics |
| 2 | **Document historical significance** | 1938 opening, "Where the Boys Are" (1960), 88 years of media coverage → designation case file |
| 3 | **Partner with preservation non-profit** | They file the historic designation — not us. Hands stay clean. Fort Lauderdale Historical Society is the candidate |
| 4 | **Prepare HPB designation application** | Complete and ready to file. Do NOT file yet. Loaded gun on the table |
| 5 | **Build petition infrastructure** | "Save the Elbo Room" — page, social assets, media contacts. Launch window = spring break season |
| 6 | **Deep research on Brooklyn 46th LLC** | Other holdings, debt, Beach Party Pizza case, developer connections. Know their position |
| 7 | **Trademark "Elbo Room"** | Federal trademark. Brand leaves with us if worst case. Signals the property without the name is worth less |
| 8 | **Property tax strategy** | VAB petition (15-25% off), purchase price allocation ($2-4M to business assets), TRIM appeal. Target: $306K → $230-250K/yr |

### Phase 2: First Contact (Early 2028)
*Read their temperature without showing our hand.*

| # | Task | Why |
|---|------|-----|
| 1 | **CRE broker approaches Jeffrey Roberts** | Casual: "long-term tenant exploring a purchase." No number yet. Broker, not attorney — less threatening |

### Phase 3A: If They Say No → Deploy Leverage (2028-2029)
*Change the math so selling to us becomes their best option.*

| # | Task | Why |
|---|------|-----|
| 1 | **Launch petition during spring break** | Target 100K+ signatures. 88-year landmark threatened = story that writes itself |
| 2 | **City Commission engagement** | Present petition. Get commissioners on record supporting preservation |
| 3 | **File historic designation** | Non-profit files with HPB. If approved: demolition requires Certificate of Appropriateness. Developer fantasy buyer gone |
| 4 | **Wait 6 months, then re-approach** | Let them absorb: designated property, no developer buyer, public movement attached. Our number looks much better now |

### Phase 3B: If They're Open → Negotiate (2028-2030)
*Close on the best possible terms.*

| # | Task | Why |
|---|------|-----|
| 1 | **Negotiate below $17M** | Use commercial comps, not condo land comps. Assessment is $3.6M. CRE attorney drives |
| 2 | **Structure the deal** | CPA allocates purchase price to reduce tax hit. Explore seller financing (they may prefer installments for tax reasons) |
| 3 | **Due diligence** | Environmental, title, survey, zoning, building inspection. Beach Party Pizza case may reveal issues |
| 4 | **Close by 2031** | Aligns with financial model. Earlier = less sublease/mortgage overlap |

### Phase 4: Post-Purchase (2031-2036)
*Survive the tight years, then reap the rewards.*

| Period | What Happens |
|--------|-------------|
| **2031-2032** | Tight: paying sublease ($365K) + mortgage ($1.02M). Small projected losses |
| **2033** | Break-even. Revenue growth closes the gap |
| **2036** | Sublease drops off. Net income jumps to **$800K+/yr** |
| **2046** | Net income reaches **$2.7M+/yr** |
| **2051** | Mortgage paid off. Property owned free and clear |

### Phase 5: Fallback (Only If Everything Fails by 2034)

| # | Task | Why |
|---|------|-----|
| 1 | **Relocate the brand** | Name, trademarks, Heritage License network all move. Building is iconic but the brand is the asset |
| 2 | **Maximize final years** | 2034-2036: squeeze every dollar. "The original Elbo Room, est. 1938" = franchise founding mythology |
