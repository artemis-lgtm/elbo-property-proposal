The Situation
We Don't Own Our Building
The Elbo Room has been a Fort Lauderdale landmark for decades. But we're tenants, not owners. Our lease has an expiration date.

Our Lease Expires in 2036

When the lease is up, the landlord can choose not to renew -- or raise the rent to whatever they want. We'd have zero leverage. After everything we've built, we could lose the building entirely.

Right now, we pay about $320,000 per year in rent. That money goes straight to the landlord. We don't build any equity. We don't own anything. Every dollar of rent is gone forever.

If we buy the building, that changes. Instead of paying a landlord, we pay ourselves. The mortgage builds equity. The property appreciates. And nobody can ever kick us out.

The Big Picture
Why This Is Worth It
Yes, 2031-2035 will be tight. Here's why we do it anyway.

If We Don't Buy

  • Lease expires in 2036 -- landlord has all the power
  • Could be forced out entirely
  • Rent could double or triple
  • $320K/year in rent, forever, building nothing
  • No asset. No equity. No security.

If We Buy

  • We own our location permanently
  • Nobody can kick us out, ever
  • Rent goes to zero in 2036
  • Building equity from Day 1
  • Own a $17M+ property outright by 2051

After 2036, We're in the Clear

Once the lease drops off, net income jumps back above $1 million per year and keeps growing. The 5 tough years buy us permanent ownership of the most iconic bar on Fort Lauderdale Beach.
